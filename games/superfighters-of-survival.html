Superfighters of Survival is a last man standing game developed in Unreal Engine 5. I had the opportunity to be the main VFX artist, as well as being in charge of helping the art team integrate character and weapon models, animations, shaders and materials. I also contributed as a gameplay and UI programmer.

Here are some VFX I made for Superfighters of Survival:

VFX Breakdown

In this section I will go into detail about the creative process behind some of the VFX in Superfighters of Survival.

Water impact

The water impact effect consists of 5 elements: water splashes, blobs and droplets, bubbles and a spiky ring.

Water splashes, blobs and droplets

To create the water splashes, blobs and droplets I procedurally generated some organic meshes using Houdini. I also generated a texture using Substance Designer.

Bubbles

I added some bubbles to be more in line with the playful design of the pool noodle weapon. The bubbles use a spherical mesh with a material created by reusing parts of the water shader used for the scene, simplifying it to make it lighter.

Spiky ring

Although this VFX is quite light hearted, it still needs to represent a weapon impact that deals damage. Taking this into account, I added a fast flashing light blue ring with spikes. To make it stand out even more, I added some contrast with a dark ring that renders behind it. The spiky ring texture was generated in Substance Designer.

Sword impact

The sword impact VFX uses a big horizontal lens flare to accompanied by smaller randomly rotated flares continued by fast sparks in order to create a sharp, bright hit effect. The lens flare texture has also been generated using Substance Designer.

Fire

The fire effect uses a blob mesh generated in Houdini, eroding it as the particle gets older. Both the flame and smoke particles use the same mesh, with different color palettes.

The same asset has been used to make the dash dust VFX.
